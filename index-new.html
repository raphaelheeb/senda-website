Our approach

We set out to build the bike we couldn't find.

Precise without being cold. Beautiful without being loud. Built with the riders who will ride it.

Switzerland stands for reliability and precision — values we build into every junction, finish, and decision. SENDA was conceived in Zürich by cyclists who believed the market had stopped surprising them.

Engadin Valley, Switzerland

Three things
we won't
compromise on.

The cycling industry makes trade-offs that riders didn't ask for. We're building a bike around the things that matter — and nothing else.

01 ——

Engineering clarity

Every frame junction is designed with structural intent. Carbon lay-up tuned for real-world conditions — efficient on climbs, composed on descents. Nothing added without reason.

02 ——

Calm aesthetics

Clean lines, balanced proportions, satin finish. A visual language shaped by Swiss design culture — restrained, timeless, and considered. Less, but better.

03 ——

Direct relationship

No distribution chain. No retail margin. Founding riders help shape the geometry and spec — and receive priority access before public launch.

The frame

Designed in Switzerland.
Built to last.

The frame geometry is defined by purposeful transitions — each angle and radius considered for stiffness, compliance, and flow. Carbon lay-up is tuned for the roads and gravel that shaped the design.
